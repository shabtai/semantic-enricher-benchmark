Proof, on a cadence

Bug of the Month

A “2-year” survival endpoint that was really 17 months

A 2025 Nature Genetics study claimed a spatial signature predicts 2-year progression-free survival in non-small-cell lung cancer and identifies patients who outperform standard-of-care immunotherapy. Our engine flagged the survival table's months column: it had been built with the wrong days-per-month divisor — 22.11 instead of 30.44 — so the advertised “2-year” endpoint is actually 17.4 months (1.45 years), silently stretching every survival figure by 37%.

3.8 → 2.2Hazard ratio, once corrected
p 0.004 → 0.11Headline significance — lost
SoC ≈ signatureMatches standard care, doesn't beat it

A single mis-computed column quietly inverted the paper's central claim — and it was caught automatically, not by a reviewer. This is exactly the class of silent defect that derails research and AI models downstream. Full public write-up available.
